Tests to Diagnose Alzheimer's Disease

Certain gene mutations can indicate a higher risk of developing Alzheimer's.

Early Onset Alzheimer's Gene Mutations

Early Onset Alzheimer's makes up 10% of all people with Alzheimer's disease.


The three single-gene mutations associated with early-onset Alzheimer's disease are:

  • Amyloid precursor protein (APP) on chromosome 21
  • Presenilin 1 (PSEN1) on chromosome 14
  • Presenilin 2 (PSEN2) on chromosome 1

People with down syndrome carry a greater risk of developing Alzheimer's because they are born with an extra chromosome with carries the APP gene mutation.

There is no blood test that can predict 100% if a person gets Alzheimer's rather one that shows your risk of developing it.
