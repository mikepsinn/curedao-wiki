Quantified Self Aggregators

Clinical Trial Related Companies

WebMD, founded in 1995, helps 156 million unique monthly visitors take an active role in managing their health by providing objective healthcare information and lifestyle information. They also make it easier for physicians and healthcare professionals to access clinical reference sources, stay abreast of the latest clinical information, learn about new treatment options, earn continuing medical education credits and communicate with peers.  WebMD is financed by advertising, third-party contributions and sponsorships. They saw $570 million in revenues in 2014.

Curetogether launched in July 2008 with 3 conditions – endometriosis, migraine, and vulvodynia. Since then, it has expanded to 185 conditions submitted by members. Their Members can check off and rate symptoms they have, treatments they’ve tried, and what they think causes or triggers their condition.

Moodscope allows users to self-report mood, which is then displayed over time.

PatientsLikeMe is a patient-powered research network with 250,000 users that provides a crowdsourced research platform. In 2013, they saw $1.4 million in revenues, primarily from the sale of user data. PatientsLikeMe has raised $27 million.

MedHelp, founded in 1994, helps 12.5 million monthly uniques and 2 million registered users to achieve better health. With communities providing support for more than 300 conditions, forums that connect consumers to doctors across the country, and partnerships with 7 of the 10 top U.S. hospitals.

Inspire is a platform for patient communities which has over 500,000 registered users worldwide, 170 online communities, and 700,000 unique visitors monthly. 

TicTrac 

is a Lifestyle Design platform that lets users discover more about themselves from the data they’re creating. Tictrac combines various forms of personal and 3rd party data, providing users with ‘personal analytics’ tools to gain a better understanding of themselves.
